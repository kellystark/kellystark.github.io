The UX Process

The main goal in UX design is for your product to give a customer the "feels", a sort of content where after using they are at peace with the design, usability and access of the product. The last thing you would want is for your customer walking away with feelings of ill-will at using your product again.

To achieve the positive "feels" for an end product, use UX Design.

UX is a process that can be broken down into steps to achieve the ultimate package for an end result, and that end result would be an overall picture of the possible user and what would please them.

What is User Interface Design (UI)?

There are many

User interface design (abbreviated as UI) is a combination of technical ability, understanding of HTML/CSS/jQuery/JavaScript, and design, to produce an end product that is not only capable of functioning in a browser/device, but is also asthetically pleasing to the eye.

Combine the Two

Both elements are crucial to a product and work closely together. Combine them and the result you achieve from contributing to front-end design, implementation, and refinement of a user interface, will be UX-traordinary.

"A UI without UX is like a painter slapping paint onto canvas without thought; while UX without UI is like the frame of a sculpture with no paper mache on it. A great product experience starts with UX followed by UI. Both are essential for the product’s success.”  Rahul Varshney
